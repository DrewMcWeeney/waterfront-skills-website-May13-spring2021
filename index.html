Course Overview and Objectives


Welcome to the Waterfront Skills module.

In this module, you will learn about the unique characteristics of waterfronts, how to use rescue watercraft, as well as waterfront-specific rescue skills. You will also learn about boating safety and the appropriate use of personal flotation devices (PFDs).


  • How do lifeguards deal with changing waterfront conditions?
  • How do lifeguards conduct facility safety checks at a waterfront?
  • What are the methods, techniques and processes for lifeguarding a waterfront?
  • What are rip currents?
  • What are some specific rules that pertain to a waterfront facility?
  • How do lifeguards swim test patrons at a waterfront?
  • What are the concerns about waterfront water?
  • What are the methods, techniques, and processes for classifying patrons?
  • What are buddy checks? What are buddy tags? How and why are they used?
  • What are the methods and techniques for swim testing patrons?
  • How do you use rescue watercraft to remove victims from the water?
  • How do you conduct a shallow and deep water search?
  • What are the types of rescue watercraft?
  • How do you use a mask and fins?

At the end of this course, you will be able to answer these questions.
